About Nataliey

Nataliey Bitature is the Chief of Staff of Simba Group of Companies and she is the Founder of Musana Carts, a company that provides urban street vendors with cleaner, safer and more efficient solutions to their cooking, storage and financing needs. Nataliey has experience across East Africa working with The Simba Group overseeing Real Estate Management, Hospitality and Energy projects. In 2016 she was named by the World Economic Forum as a Top African Innovator; 2017 presented at the World Bank Spring Meetings and in 2018 was named in Forbes’ 30 Under 30 for Africa.

Nataliey is launching Ener-Grow, a productive asset financing company for rural entrepreneurs offering necessary business and financial training programs. They are building their model to work hand in hand with, and benefit from, the expansion of the electricity grid to rural areas. Through partnerships with banks, their customers will gain access to the capital markets and as such cheaper loans and assets, whilst banks gain hard-to-reach rural customers whilst assuming zero risk.

She has an undergraduate degree in Business Management and Education from Keele University, UK and a Masters of Social Entrepreneurship from Hult International Business School, San Francisco.

Nataliey's Work

Musana means ‘Sun’ in Luganda. The Musana Cart is an innovative technology that includes an eco-friendly stove (that reduces smoke emission and fire hazards), a small refrigerator and light bulbs. Everything is powered by a solar panel, eliminating the need for charcoal.

learn more

Furthermore, adding a solar eco stove to a vendor’s street cart eliminates the need for fuel, reducing expenses. The energy can also be used to power electronics including; refrigerators, warmers and a host of other electronics.

learn more

Musana prides itself in providing an environment that is clean, hospitable and customer friendly giving it a competitive advantage over other street vendors. This a built a stronger relationship between vendors and customers.

learn more

The fact that the cart is powered by a solar panel it enables the vendor to make a higher profit margin since they do not have to pay for energy. Musana also provides vendors with access to finance and business training.
